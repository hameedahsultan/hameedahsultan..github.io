About me

I am a Ph.D. student in the School of Information Technology, IIT Delhi since 2015. I am working under the guidance of Dr. Smruti R. Sarangi. Before that I have completed my Master's degree (M.Tech.) in VLSI Design Tools and Technology from IIT Delhi in 2015 and Bachelor's in Electronics Engineering (B.Tech.) from Aligarh Muslim University in 2013.

My research areas are system-level thermal modeling, low power design, and machine learning for CAD. I am especially interested in Green's function-based methods and leakage-aware thermal modeling.
A description of the projects that I have worked on can be found here. My list of publications can be found here.

Throughout my academic career, I have been fortunate to be among the top performers in the class. I have been the gold medalist in my Bachelor's, and the top ranker in the entire VLSI batch in my Master's program. One of my papers was nominated for the best paper award at ICCAD 2019. I have also been awarded the Outstanding Teaching Assistant award for my role as a TA in the course COL100: Introduction to Computer Science. I was the head TA, responsible for managing a team of 28 TAs in a class of 474 students. I have coordinated another large course as the head TA - Computer Architecture (192 students, 10 TAs).
I have also mentored four master's students towards their thesis.
